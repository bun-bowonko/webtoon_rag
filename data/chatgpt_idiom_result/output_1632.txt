3. 간이 크다 > Have a big liver (Be very bold)  
4. 간이 콩알만 해지다 > Liver becomes the size of a bean (Be extremely scared)  
5. 같은 값이면 다홍치마 > If the price is the same, choose the red skirt (Choose the better option if the price is the same)  
6. 개같이 벌어 정승같이 쓴다 > Earn like a dog, spend like a nobleman (Work hard and spend wisely)  
7. 개천에서 용 난다 > A dragon rises from a small stream (A great person comes from humble beginnings)  
8. 걱정도 팔자다 > Worrying is also fate (Worrying too much over unnecessary things)  
9. 겉 다르고 속 다르다 > Outside is different from inside (Be two-faced)  
10. 고기를 잡으려면 물에 들어가야 한다 > To catch fish, you have to go into the water (Take risks to achieve something)  
11. 고슴도치도 제 새끼는 예쁘다 > Even a hedgehog finds its child pretty (Parents always think their children are the best)  
12. 공든 탑이 무너지랴 > A carefully built tower won’t collapse (Hard work pays off)  
13. 구관이 명관이다 > The old official is the wise one (Experienced people are more reliable)  
14. 구슬이 서 말이라도 꿰어야 보배 > Even if you have three bushels of pearls, you must thread them to have treasure (Potential is meaningless without effort)  
15. 그림의 떡 > A rice cake in a picture (Something you desire but can't have)  
16. 금강산도 식후경 > Even Mount Geumgang must be seen after eating (Eating comes first before anything)  
17. 그것도 때가 있다 > There is a time for everything (Everything has its right moment)  
18. 길고 짧은 것은 대봐야 안다 > You don’t know which is longer or shorter until you compare (The result is unknown until the end)  
19. 김칫국부터 마시지 마라 > Don’t drink kimchi soup first (Don’t count your chickens before they hatch)  
20. 낮말은 새가 듣고 밤말은 쥐가 듣는다 > Birds hear words spoken in the day, rats hear words spoken at night (Be careful what you say)  
21. 남의 떡이 더 커 보인다 > Someone else's rice cake looks bigger (The grass is always greener on the other side)  
22. 낫 놓고 기역 자도 모른다 > Not knowing the letter 'ㄱ' even while looking at a sickle (Be extremely ignorant)  
23. 내 코가 석 자다 > My nose is three feet long (I have too many problems to help others)  
24. 눈 감으면 코 베어 간다 > If you close your eyes, your nose will be cut off (Be cautious, as people might take advantage of you)  
25. 눈에 넣어도 아프지 않다 > It wouldn’t hurt even if put in the eye (Extremely lovable)  
26. 다 된 밥에 재 뿌리기 > Sprinkling ashes on cooked rice (Ruining something that is already well done)

28. 돌다리도 두들겨 보고 건너라 > Even when crossing a stone bridge, tap it first (Be extra careful)  
29. 뒤통수를 맞다 > Get hit on the back of the head (Be betrayed)  
30. 등잔 밑이 어둡다 > The bottom of the lamp is dark (You’re oblivious to what’s right under your nose)  
31. 떡 줄 사람은 생각도 안 하는데 김칫국부터 마신다 > The giver isn't even thinking about giving the rice cake, but you already drank kimchi soup (Getting ahead of yourself)  
32. 마른하늘에 날벼락 > A sudden thunderbolt from a clear sky (An unexpected disaster)  
33. 말 한마디에 천 냥 빚도 갚는다 > A word can pay back a debt of a thousand nyang (Speech has great power)  
34. 목구멍이 포도청 > The throat is the police station (People do anything to survive)  
35. 믿는 도끼에 발등 찍힌다 > The axe you trust cuts your foot (Betrayed by someone you trust)  
36. 바늘 가는 데 실 간다 > Where the needle goes, the thread follows (Two things are always together)

41. 보기 좋은 떡이 먹기도 좋다 > A rice cake that looks good also tastes good (First impressions matter)  
42. 병 주고 약 준다 > Give the disease and then the cure (Act nice after harming someone)

48. 숭어가 뛰니까 망둥이도 뛴다 > The goby jumps because the mullet jumps (Copying the talented without ability)  
49. 식은 죽 먹기 > Eating cold porridge (Very easy task)

53. 앞뒤가 꽉 막히다 > Blocked from front and back (Narrow-minded)
